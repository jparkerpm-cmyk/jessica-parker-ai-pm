Day 2 — lower body (belt off during work block)
Stop the pad at 2:00. Bands stack on top of dumbbells for extra glute load, or replace them on lighter days — both work.
WalkWarmup, belt on2 min
Belt off1 min each5 min
01
Goblet squat
Quads, glutes
Dumbbells
Hold one dumbbell vertically at chest, squat deep, elbows inside knees
Short loop band
Band just above knees, squat and press knees out against band — loads glutes harder than dumbbells at the top
02
Romanian deadlift
Hamstrings, glutes
Dumbbells
Soft bend in knees, hinge at hips, weights track down legs, feel the hamstring stretch
Long band
Stand on band with both feet, hold ends, hinge same as RDL — resistance increases as you stand back up
03
Reverse lunge
Quads, glutes, balance
Dumbbells
Hold at sides, step back, lower rear knee toward floor, alternate legs
Short loop band
Band just above knees — adds hip abductor challenge and keeps front knee tracking over foot
04
Sumo squat
Inner thighs, glutes
Dumbbells
Wide stance, toes out, hold one dumbbell with both hands, squat deep
Short loop band
Band above knees, wide stance, press knees out against band the whole way down and up
05
Single-leg deadlift
Hamstrings, glutes, balance
Dumbbells
Hold in one or both hands, hinge on one leg, rear leg lifts behind, alternate sides
Long band
Stand on band with one foot, hold end with same side hand, hinge — adds balance challenge

Lower body days: at 2:00 stop the belt. Do all 5 exercises standing still. Restart at 7:00.
Starting resistance
Dumbbells upper body: 3–5 lb per hand. Dumbbells lower body: 10–15 lb per hand. Bands: start with lightest loop you have. Walking adds metabolic load — go lighter than expected week one.
Progression (weeks 4–6)
Upper
Add 2 lb or move to a heavier band when 3 circuits feel easy
Lower
Add 5 lb or heavier band when form stays solid through all 3 circuits
Speed
Add 0.5 mph to walk blocks when ready
